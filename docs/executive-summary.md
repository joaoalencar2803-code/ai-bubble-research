# Executive Summary

This research examines whether the unprecedented wave of investment in Artificial Intelligence infrastructure represents a sustainable technological transformation or a speculative capital allocation bubble.

The thesis argues that AI has genuine long-term economic value, while questioning whether current capital expenditures, corporate financing structures, and market valuations are consistent with realistic future cash flows.

The analysis integrates accounting, corporate finance, macroeconomics, market structure, energy constraints, and systemic risk to evaluate the resilience of the AI ecosystem.

Key topics include:

- Capital expenditure trends
- GPU economics
- Cloud infrastructure
- Corporate interdependencies
- Accounting practices
- Private credit financing
- Energy constraints
- Historical comparisons with previous infrastructure bubbles
- Quantitative downside scenarios
